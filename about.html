About Us

Last Updated: July 14, 2026

Welcome to SSC Sign, a dedicated, free online utility developed to assist government job aspirants across India in formatting and compressing their photo and signature files for online registration portals.

Our Mission

Every year, millions of Indian competitive exam candidates apply for recruitment posts through portals like the Staff Selection Commission (SSC), Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), banking (IBPS), railways (RRB), and state-level exams. One of the most common hurdles candidates face is the strict image criteria: files must be within narrow kilobyte thresholds (e.g., exactly 10 KB to 20 KB) and meet precise physical ratios (e.g., 6.0 cm × 2.0 cm at 300 DPI).

Many candidates do not have access to professional desktop image editors (like Photoshop) or find them too complex. They often resort to paid cyber cafes or questionable third-party websites that upload sensitive files to their remote servers. SSC Sign was built to bridge this gap: providing a simple, professional-grade, and 100% secure image resizer that runs entirely locally inside your browser.

Why SSC Sign is Different

  • Serverless Client-Side Architecture: We prioritize your security. Unlike other online converters, we do not upload your images to any database or web server. All cropping, filtering, and compression computations are executed locally on your device's web browser using HTML5 Canvas technology.
  • Automatic Paper Whitening Filter: Signature photos taken on mobile phones often capture yellow room lighting, gray table backdrops, or shadows. Our built-in whitening contrast filter separates dark ink strokes from paper fibers, generating a clean, high-contrast, pure white background acceptable to portal validators.
  • Preserves Official Density Markers: We insert standard density header tags directly into the JPEG binary files, ensuring the metadata reflects the correct 300 DPI resolution, helping you pass portal validation checks.
  • Binary Search Size Targeting: Finding the right image quality parameter to hit a 10-20 KB file size manually is tedious. Our engine runs an automated binary search compression loop, executing multiple compression attempts in milliseconds to maximize resolution while keeping the file strictly in the target size range.
